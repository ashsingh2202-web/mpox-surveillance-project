# Mpox Surveillance Project – Florida

## Project Timeline
Fall 2024 | Florida Department of Health Internship

## Project Overview
This project analyzes reported Mpox cases in Florida to identify demographic trends, high-risk populations, and public health disparities. The objective was to support targeted outreach and surveillance planning efforts.

## Objectives
- Examine demographic distribution of reported cases
- Identify high-risk age groups
- Assess disparities among Hispanic populations
- Generate visual summaries for epidemiologic reporting

## Tools & Software
- SAS (Data cleaning and statistical analysis)
- Microsoft Excel (Data organization and visualization)
- Epidemiologic surveillance methodology

## Methods
A descriptive epidemiologic analysis was conducted on reported Mpox cases. Data were stratified by:
- Age group
- Sex
- Ethnicity
- Geographic region

Trend visualization and frequency distributions were generated to identify patterns.

## Key Findings
- Increased burden observed among Hispanic populations
- Adults aged 25–44 represented the highest proportion of cases
- Urban counties showed higher case clustering

## Public Health Impact
Findings informed targeted community outreach strategies and supported surveillance reporting at the state level.

## Ethical Considerations
All data used were de-identified and analyzed in accordance with public health surveillance standards.
